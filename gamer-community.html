01

Project Context: The Retention Leak

Newegg is the go-to destination for high-performance components, yet it struggled with customer retention post-purchase. Users viewed the platform as a transactional pass-through — once the parts arrived, the relationship ended.

My mission was to complete the Newegg ecosystem by extending the user journey from 'Buying and Building' to 'Showcasing and Competing.'

"I approached this as both a designer and a World Builder. I applied the principle of 留白 — the intentional void — to ensure that amidst high-density technical specs, the interface provided the 'breathing room' for a community to thrive."

03

The Problem: The "Ghost Town" Bottleneck

The existing Showcase was outdated and overly technical, leading to community stagnation. While users were still posting monthly, engagement was nearly non-existent. The Product Management team requested a UI facelift — but I identified a deeper architectural flaw: the platform lacked a social 'reason to stay' after the build was complete.

04

Business Concerns & Skepticism

My Concern

During kickoff, I grew skeptical that a UI modernization alone could revive the platform, given that engagement rates had historically remained under 30%. I challenged the brief: we didn't just need new pixels — we needed a new Product Strategy to bridge the gap between technical utility and human interaction.

05

Research: Diagnosing the Cultural Physics

"To build a world, you must understand its laws. I used AI-assisted behavioral analysis to map the motivators of top contributors on Reddit and Discord. I discovered that while builders care about specs, they are driven by reputation, humor, and showing off. I benchmarked competitors like PC Part Picker and Reddit to identify the 'Aha!' moments in content discovery and reward systems."

06

Key Insight: The Builder–Gamer Intersection

Research revealed a massive 'Engagement Bottleneck': hardware discussions are often transactional — once a problem is fixed, the conversation dies.

07

The Discovery & Strategic Shift

The Discovery

"A significant portion of 'Builders' are actually 'Gamers' optimizing for performance. By introducing gamers into the builder ecosystem, we provided the social adrenaline needed to keep the community active post-purchase."

I scheduled a follow-up meeting with the product management and tech team to showcase my findings and successfully pivot the project — upgrading both the technical Showcase and launching a standalone Gamer Community Forum.

✦
The Strategic Shift
Upgrade the PC Build Showcase with better usability and social affordances
Launch a standalone Gamer Community Forum (open-source MVP), with PC Build Showcase as a category within it
Introduce gamers into the builder ecosystem — providing the social layer to sustain engagement post-purchase

Part 1: Improved PC Build Showcase Forum

By prioritizing surgical clarity and high-fidelity visual storytelling, I transformed the PC Build Showcase into a digital workshop that celebrates technical craftsmanship. The interface leverages immersive, "blown-up" visuals to honor the user's fruit of labor, while deploying structured filters that cater to the surgical precision required by goal-oriented builders.

This is where the technical rigor of the "World" is established — moving the platform away from a static gallery and into a high-utility environment where every component is documented with intent.
